Adventures in Mac Programming

About Me

Hi, I’m Casey.

I love the Mac and have always aspired to build native software for it. I’m currently employed as a senior full-stack engineer using web technologies at FSB Companies, and I previously worked at Apple on similar technology stacks. While building for the web has been interesting over the years, my heart has always been with the Mac.

Thus far in my career, the extent of my native programming for Apple platforms could be described (at best) as intermittent dabbling. As I now make a more serious effort to become a skilled macOS developer, this blog is intended to chronicle my journey. Hopefully there are people out there with similar backgrounds in web technologies who I can inspire to consider targeting the Mac, taking advantage of all its unique capabilities.

You can follow my professional journey over at Native Crane, where I build native software for Apple platforms.

Why Objective-C(asey)?

Objective-C is a programming language developed in the 1980s with a long history of being used on Apple platforms. In 2014, Apple released Swift, a new programming language that is strongly positioned to take the reigns from Objective-C’s dominance.

So... Why not Swift-Casey? Well, for one, it ruins the pun. And two, Objective-C simply has a more Mac-focused history compared to Swift, which is something I find pretty cool. (And three, maybe I'm just an old dinosaur.)

Despite the name, I intend to feature a range of content related to both Swift and Objective-C, among other interesting technical and non-technical topics.
